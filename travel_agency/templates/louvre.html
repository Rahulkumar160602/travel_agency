The Louvre Museum

  1. The Louvre Museum is located in Paris, France. It is the largest and one of the most famous art museums in the world, attracting millions of visitors annually.
  2. The museum was originally built as a fortress in the late 12th century under Philip II. It was later converted into a royal palace in the 16th century before becoming a public museum during the French Revolution in 1793.
  3. The Louvre is home to an impressive collection of over 380,000 pieces of art, including paintings, sculptures, and archaeological artifacts, although only about 35,000 are displayed at any given time.
  4. The most iconic artwork at the Louvre is Leonardo da Vinci’s **Mona Lisa**, which draws millions of visitors annually. Other masterpieces include the **Venus de Milo**, **Winged Victory of Samothrace**, and Eugène Delacroix’s **Liberty Leading the People**.
  5. In 1989, the museum underwent a major renovation with the addition of the **glass pyramid entrance**, designed by architect I. M. Pei. The pyramid has become a modern symbol of the Louvre and a key part of its identity.
  6. The Louvre Museum spans over 72,735 square meters of exhibition space. It is considered a must-visit destination for art and history enthusiasts, offering an unparalleled glimpse into human creativity and culture.
